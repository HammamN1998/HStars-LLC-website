1. Scope

This Policy applies to:

  • Clinic and practitioner account holders who subscribe to ClinicWell;
  • Authorized users (staff) you invite or allow to use your account; and
  • Personal information processed through the Service about your patients, which you enter as part of using the software.

It does not apply to third-party websites or services you link to from the Service.

3. Information we collect

3.1 Account and clinic information

When you register or manage an account, we may collect:

  • Name and professional/clinic name;
  • Email address and phone number;
  • Postal or business address;
  • Profile photo and biography fields you choose to provide;
  • Login credentials (passwords are stored by our authentication provider in hashed form—we do not store plain-text passwords);
  • Subscription plan, usage limits, and billing status.

3.2 Patient and clinical information (Customer Data)

You may enter information about patients, which can include:

  • Identifiers and demographics (name, gender, date of birth, contact details, address);
  • Health-related information (e.g., blood group, blood pressure, conditions, clinical notes, treatment notes);
  • Appointment and treatment records;
  • Files and attachments (images, documents);
  • Dental charting/diagram data;
  • Internal payment/treatment balance records you log in the system.

You control what you enter. The sensitivity of this data may be high under local health-privacy laws. You must only collect and enter data you are legally permitted to process.

3.3 Payment information

We use Stripe to process subscription payments. We receive:

  • Billing name and email;
  • Subscription and transaction metadata (amount, date, plan, payment status);
  • Limited card information (e.g., last four digits, brand, expiry) from the processor—we do not receive or store full card numbers on our servers.

Payment data is also subject to Stripe’s privacy policy.

3.4 Technical and usage information

We automatically collect or receive:

  • IP address, browser type, device information;
  • Log data related to authentication, errors, and security events;
  • Cookies and similar technologies used for session management and security (see Section 10);
  • Firebase/Google analytics identifiers where enabled (e.g., measurement ID in our app configuration).

8. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect personal information, including:

  • Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS) for web traffic;
  • Access controls tied to authenticated accounts;
  • Firebase/Google Cloud security features for hosted data.

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. You are responsible for securing devices and credentials used to access your account.

Breach notification: If we become aware of a personal data breach affecting your account that we are legally required to report, we will notify you without undue delay in accordance with applicable law.

9. Your rights and choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

  • Access personal information we hold about your account;
  • Correct inaccurate account information;
  • Delete your account and associated data (subject to retention limits above);
  • Export data you have entered;
  • Object to or restrict certain processing;
  • Withdraw consent where processing is consent-based;
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (e.g., in the EU/UK).

To exercise rights regarding your HStars account: email hammam.najem@clinicwell.app with the subject “Privacy Request,” including your account email and a description of your request. We may verify your identity before responding. We aim to respond within 30 days (or as required by law).

Patient requests: Direct patients to contact your clinic. We will assist you as processor when you instruct us and when legally permitted.

10. Cookies and similar technologies

We use essential cookies and local storage technologies to:

  • Keep you signed in (Firebase Authentication sessions);
  • Maintain security and prevent fraud;
  • Remember preferences necessary for the Service to function.

We do not use advertising cookies on the core application unless we explicitly notify you and offer a choice. Our public marketing website (hstars-2025.web.app) uses Google Analytics 4 to measure aggregate traffic and interactions (such as page views, call-to-action clicks, and contact form submissions). This helps us improve the site and is not used for cross-context behavioral advertising. If we enable analytics within the core application, we will describe it here and in the app.

You can control cookies through browser settings; disabling essential cookies may prevent login.

The Service is not directed to children under 16 (or under 13 where U.S. COPPA applies) as account holders. You must not use the Service to knowingly collect personal information from children without appropriate authority and consents under your local laws.

If you enter minor patient data as part of clinical practice, you are responsible for lawful basis and parental/guardian authority as required in your jurisdiction.

12. Do not sell or share (U.S. state privacy laws)

We do not sell personal information. We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as defined under applicable U.S. state privacy laws.

13. Changes to this Policy
